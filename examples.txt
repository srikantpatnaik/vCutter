
1.Convert/Encode inVideo to outVideo.Must give extension to OutVideo.
$ vcutter 1 -i inVideo.ogv -o outVideo.webm
      
2.Add(-a) a video at the beginning(-b) or end(-e) of other video and stich
  them as output(-o) video. 
$ vcutter 2 -a smallClip.ogv -b  OrigVideo.ogv -o smallClipOrigVideo.ogv
                (inVideo)         (inVideo)         (outVideo)

3.Cut and replace clip from testVideo at duration (hh:mm:ss) to (hh:mm:yy) and insert
  newClip in place of it, stich them as newVideo(-o) video.
$ vcutter 3 -c testVideo.ogv -t 23:59:55.00 23:59:59.00 -I newClip.ogv -o newVideo.ogv 
               (inVideo)                                  (inVideo)      (outVideo)
       
4.Cut(-c) the clip from (hh:mm:ss) to (hh:mm:yy) duration of testVideo and stich back 
  the remaining portion as output(-o) video.
$ vcutter 4 -c testVideo.ogv -t 23:59:55.00 23:59:59.00 -o newVideo.ogv 
                (inVideo)                                  (outVideo) 
        
5.Cut(-c) from (hh:mm:ss) to (hh:mm:yy) duration of testVideo and save cut clip as newVideo. 
$ vcutter 5 -c testVideo.ogv -t1 23:59:55.00 23:59:59.00 -o newVideo.ogv 
                (inVideo)                                   (outVideo)
                
6.Covert all videos in a directory to certain format.
$ vcutter 6 sourceDir -f mp4  destDir
            (inVideo)       (outVideo)
Source dir must contain all videos in same format.
        
Please note, cut operation doesn't effect the original video.Your original
video remains intact.
